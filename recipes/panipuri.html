Pani Puri

A deep-fried, hollow, spherical shell that's about 1 inch in diameter. It's filled with a combination of potatoes, chickpeas, raw onions, and spices, and topped with a spicy and tangy water, usually tamarind or mint.

Ingredients

  • Semolina (suji)
  • Wheat flour
  • Salt
  • Baking soda
  • Water
  • Boiled potatoes smashed
  • Boiled chickpeas
  • Chaat masala
  • Mint leaves

Instructions.

  1. Prepare the Puri
    • Mix semolina and all-purpose flour in a bowl.
    • Add water gradually and knead into a stiff dough.
    • Cover and let it rest for 20 minutes.
    • Roll out thin puris and cut them into small circles.
    • Heat oil and deep fry the puris until golden and puffed.
    • Cool them completely and store in an airtight container.
  2. Prepare the Pani
    • Blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chili, ginger, tamarind pulp, cumin powder, chaat masala, black salt, and regular salt into a smooth paste.
    • Mix the paste with chilled water.
    • Adjust salt and tanginess as needed. Refrigerate until use.
  3. Assemble Pani Puri
    • Gently crack open the top of a puri.
    • Fill it with potato stuffing.
    • Pour the spicy pani into the puri.
    • Serve immediately and enjoy!
